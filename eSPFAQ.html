  1. What does "eSP" stand for?
    eSP is an acronym for enmail Service Provider
  1. What does "enmail" stand for?
    The prefix "en" means "my" in Tamil language; so enmail means "my mail", or as we'd like to say "its' e-mail with personality"
  1. What services does enmail provide?
    We provide Internet e-mail services for businesses of all sizes. We are indispensable for any company that needs Internet e-mail.

    Currently our services include domain management, web-based e-mail, POP3 access, SPAM prevention, etc. Some of our upcoming services could be Virus scanning of attachments, unified messaging, policy management, etc.
  1. What's the difference between enmail and other Internet Service Providers?
    We are unique in developing indigenous technology required for large-scale e-mail systems. Our distributed cluster architecture ensures that our services are affordable, scaleable, and secure. Having control over the technology gives us the ability to customize the services to suit the specific requirements of our customers.

    Our business focus is to provide e-mail/messaging and related services only. Our mandate is to hide the complexities of technology and allow the common man's business to take advantage of the Internet.
  1. Who can become an eSP?
    Anyone who has the passion to help companies do business on the Internet and make money in the process.
  1. What's the business opportunity?
    Every company needs to be on the Internet in the next few years or risk going out of business. It is no longer optional, but mandatory. The market opportunity for Internet enabling companies is just starting to happen.

    There exists a requirement for companies that deliver value to customers, by hiding the complexities of technology and allowing them to take advantage of the Internet.
  1. How does it help my business?
    As the margins for products in the computer industry continues to fall, it is becoming more difficult for computer dealers to remain profitable.

    Becoming an eSP, allows dealers to enter into the Internet business. By aiding existing customers to Internet enable their companies, a new source of revenue is added to the bottom line.
  1. Do we get trained for this?
    Absolutely, we provide FREE training for all our eSPs on an ongoing basis.
  1. What's the market-size?
    Any business that has PCs and an Internet connection is a potential customer. In a city like Chennai, there are over 50,000 companies that need an Internet presence. This represents a conservative estimate of Rs. 50 Crores per annum.
  1. What do we have to invest to get started?

  1. What kind of returns can an eSP expect?

  1. What happens when two eSPs approach the same customer?
