ENMAIL.COM ANNOUNCES INVESTMENT FROM INTEL CAPITAL
Chennai, 27th February 2001
 
enmail.com Private Limited (enmail), an application service provider (ASP) of email and messaging services, announced today that it has received an investment from Intel Capital.The investment will help enmail.com expand its operations and marketing capabilities in India.

enmail offers simple, safe and secure customized email services to small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). This helps SMEs establish an Internet presence without having to employ in-house expertise. Companies are also able to engage in business-to-business e-commerce through enmail.com.

Discussing the investment, Karthik Iyer, chief executive officer of enmail.com explained, "Our technology and services are presented in a business-friendly manner, which enables quicker acceptance of the Internet in the SME market segment. This investment will help expand enmail further across India by strengthening our marketing and infrastructure programs."

"Intel Capital invests in companies, such as enmail.com, that are growing the Internet economy," said Avtar Saini, Director of South Asia, Intel. "With companies like enmail that provide email and messaging services, SMEs can now incorporate the Internet into their business and enjoy the advantages of e-Business."

About enmail.com
Founded in March 2000, enmail.com focuses on establishing Internet presence for SMEs and providing outsourced email services for portals and Internet Service Providers. The company targets the large segment of Indian corporates that have been unable to leverage the advantages of the Internet due to lack of knowledge and/or resources. Additional information is available at http://www.enmail.com

About Intel Capital
Intel Capital Intel's strategic investment program, focuses on making equity investments and acquisitions to grow the Internet economy, including Internet infrastructure, content and services in support of Intel's strategic interests. In India, Intel Capital invests through Intel Pacific, Inc., a subsidiary of Intel Corporation.
