  Get Your Company on the Internet
  		 
 
Everybody seems to be talking about the Internet, so what exactly is it? The Internet is a computer network that connects people all across the world. It is not owned or controlled by any single organization or government. The spirit of the Internet is to be open and share information with the world. 

The Internet is fast becoming the preferred medium of communication for businesses of all sizes. Having an Internet Presence is no longer a luxury, but a necessity for most companies.

While large companies can afford to hire a dedicated team of specialists to design, build, and exploit the commercial aspects of the Internet, smaller companies are often confused and intimidated by their perception of what it takes to get their company on the net.

Actually the process of getting your company on the Internet is quite simple and can be done in 4 simple steps: 
  1. Choose an Internet address
  2. Create e-mail accounts
  3. Create web-pages for your web site and
  4. Find an Web Service Provider to host the services.

Internet Address
To get an Internet address that symbolizes the name of a company involves a process known as "Domain Name Registration" or DNS in short. Similar to performing a name search and registering a company with the Registrar of Companies, one has to go through an Internet name search and get it registered through an Internet Service Provider (ISP). 

While there are several options available, getting a "<companyname>.com" address is the most preferred. Because of the popularity of the Internet most of the common .com names have already been registered.

Another option is for companies to register a sub-domain name, ("<companyname>.<ispname>.com") where the <companyname> is actually the sub-domain of <ispname>.com.

While domain registration typically costs about US $ 70 for 2 years, sub domain registrations are usually free.

The Internet address provides you with an internet identity. It's importance is understood only when used with services such as e-mail (electronic mail), WWW (World Wide Web), ftp (File Transfer Protocol) etc.

e-mail
e-mail is the foundation upon which the Internet is built. With e-mail services a company would typically provide e-mail ids' such as <user>@<companyname>.com to its employees to facilitate official communication with the rest of the world. In general, companies have an info e-mail account, info@<companyname>.com,  that is analogous to the main telephone number.

Web site
The Internet as we know today is vastly associated with the World Wide Web. It is a service (similar to e-mail) that companies use to share information. It allows a company to post information (web pages) about its products/services for access by anybody on the Internet. The web pages are formatted in an English-like computer language known as HTML (Hyper Text Markup Language).

The web site of a company is accessed by its Internet address, such as www.<companyname>.com.

Having a web site today is fashionable.But, very few companies are taking full advantage of the rich medium offered by the Internet. A company that is serious about its Internet presence, needs to put in a lot of thought and effort in its web site on a continual basis.

Internet Hosting
Services, such as e-mail and WWW, are accessible only when they are hosted on a server that is permanently connected to the Internet. While there are lots of software that provide these functionalities to any computer, being on the internet opens up issues such as security, reliability, backup & restore, etc.

For a fee, ISPs (Internet Service Providers) typically provide the appropriate infrastructure along with management of such services. These services, as provided by ISPs, are typically known as web site hosting, e-mail hosting, network management, etc.
